Goal:
Generate a collision-safe survey path for greenhouse racks and overwrite `waypoints.txt`.

Inputs:
1) `greenhouse_map.pgm`
2) `greenhouse_map.yaml` (resolution + origin)

Coordinate/units:
- Use ROS `map` frame coordinates (meters).
- Heading is `yaw_deg` (degrees, ROS yaw about +Z).
- Direction convention: 0=+X, 90=+Y, -90=-Y, +/-180=-X.

Mission requirements:
- Start waypoint must be exactly: `0.00, 1.15, 0.50, 0.0`
- End waypoint must be exactly: `0.00, 1.15, 0.50, 0.0`
- Survey all rack faces that point toward map center/origin side.
- Maintain at least 0.20 m clearance from static obstacles (treat map obstacles as inflated by 0.20 m).
- Keep all waypoints in free space and inside map bounds.
- Consecutive waypoint spacing should be 0.20 to 0.30 m (allow up to 0.35 m only when necessary).
- No duplicate consecutive waypoints.
- Heading is required for every waypoint.
- On survey segments, heading must fully face the rack

Body-frame / camera convention:
- The drone camera optical axis is aligned with the drone +X body axis.
- `yaw_deg` sets camera look direction in the map frame.

Heading requirements for survey waypoints:
- Heading must align with the inward rack-face normal (camera +X points from aisle into the rack face), not just any perpendicular.
- Do not use tangential headings along the rack face for survey waypoints.
- Keep this facing direction consistent along each straight survey pass (avoid unnecessary yaw flips).

Output format (`waypoints.txt` only):
- Optional comments may start with `#`.
- Waypoint rows must be exactly: `x, y, z, yaw_deg`
- Use two decimals for x/y/z and one decimal for yaw.
- Example: `-1.35, -0.48, 0.50, 90.0`

Output rules:
- Overwrite `waypoints.txt`.
- Do not print explanations or extra prose.
- Only write the file content.
